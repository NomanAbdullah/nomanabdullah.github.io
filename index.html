About Me

Welcome to my academic homepage! I am a PhD student in Computer Science specializing in cybersecurity, working under the supervision of Dr. Mengfei Ren on cutting-edge security research. My primary focus is on conducting comprehensive security analysis of C compilers and developing innovative approaches to enhance software security at the compiler level.

I am particularly passionate about the intersection of compiler design and security engineering, with the goal of creating more secure software ecosystems through compiler-based protections. My research encompasses static analysis techniques, memory safety enhancements, and security-preserving compiler optimizations that maintain both performance and robustness against various attack vectors.

My work contributes to the broader field of software security by addressing fundamental vulnerabilities at the compilation stage, with applications spanning from IoT devices to enterprise systems. I am actively engaged in research collaborations and am working towards publications in top-tier security conferences and journals.
